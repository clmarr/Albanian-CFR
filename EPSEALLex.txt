$tërsirë/ Gheg tërsinë -- excluded. Jokl 1916(106-7) apud Curtis 2012(:102) has it attributed to a PSl torčina which is supposed to give Bulgarian trǎsina and the Romanian item that is variously târsână, târsină and tărșânâ. However the direction of loaning is unclear; Romanian has been attributed to Bulgarian, which would not regularly reflect the P-Slavic; a Bulgarian reflex of a Latin transenna has also been proposed; Albanian has been "blamed" on Romanian by Orel 1998: 455, who also argues the rhotacism is analogical. Omari & Ylli 2023 are also sceptical.
